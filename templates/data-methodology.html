CNI ANALYSIS

What are CNI parameters?

CNI Model Parameters

The CNI model breaks down moral judgments into three distinct parameters:

C - Consequences

Utilitarian thinking

Sensitivity to the outcomes and consequences of your actions when you deem them for the sake of greater good. The closer the C score is to 1, the less likely you are to make the choice to sacrifice something, even when the benefits of action are greater than the cost.

N - Norms

Deontological thinking

Sensitivity to moral norms and the general rule of not harming people and caring for them. The closer the N score is to 1, the less likely you are to support an action when a norm prohibits it.

I - Inaction

Action bias

General bias toward inaction versus action. The closer the I score is to 1, the less likely you are to intervene in any situation whether there are good or bad consequences, whether the norms presrcibs it or no.

LLM PROFILES

Creating Artificial Agents

The key part of my project was to generate artificial agents using the LLaMA-3 open-source LLM. Each profile was created from the Big Five personality traits of a real participant from the Luke and Gawronski dataset.

The Big Five Personality Traits

Openness

Imagination, curiosity, willingness to try new experiences

Conscientiousness

Organization, dependability, goal-directed behavior

Extraversion

Sociability, assertiveness, emotional expressiveness

Agreeableness

Compassion, cooperation, trust in others

Neuroticism

Emotional stability, tendency toward negative emotions
