What are CNI parameters?

CNI Model Parameters

The CNI model breaks down moral judgments into three distinct parameters between O and 1:

C - Consequences

Utilitarian thinking

Sensitivity to the outcomes and consequences of your actions when you deem them for the sake of greater good. The closer the C score is to 1, the less likely you are to make the choice to sacrifice something, even when the benefits of action are greater than the cost.

N - Norms

Deontological thinking

Sensitivity to moral norms of not harming and caring for people. The closer the N score is to 1, the less likely you are to support an action when a norm prohibits it.

I - Inaction

Action bias

General bias toward inaction versus action. The closer the I score is to 1, the less likely you are to intervene in any situation whether there are good or bad consequences, whether the norms presrcibs it or no.
